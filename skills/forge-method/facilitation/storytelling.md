# facilitation: storytelling

purpose:
  Shape a narrative arc that changes what an audience feels, understands, believes, or does.

open_floor:
  "Who is the audience, what do they believe now, what should change, and what story would be too obvious or fake?"

source_material:
  Ask for audience, context, product or world facts, tone references, anti-references, source claims, scenes, characters, proof points, and forbidden tropes.

follow_up_batches:
  - audience_shift: "What does the audience believe before and after the story?"
  - pressure: "What tension, obstacle, contradiction, or risk makes the story worth following?"
  - voice: "What should the voice feel like, and what should it never sound like?"
  - proof: "Which claims, scenes, or facts make the story earned instead of decorative?"
  - rejection: "Which obvious arcs, tropes, and endings must stay dead?"

conversation_stages:
  - audience_state: "Name the audience, starting belief, desired shift, and stakes."
  - story_spine: "Draft beginning, pressure, turn, payoff, and after-state."
  - voice_taste: "Calibrate voice with references, anti-references, rhythm, and forbidden tones."
  - proof_bind: "Attach story beats to facts, product truths, mechanics, or character/world rules."
  - reject_paths: "Record tempting but wrong arcs so future agents do not revive them."
  - handoff: "Persist arc, voice, beats, proof, rejects, and next workflow."

elicitation_options:
  - story_spine: "Once there was, every day, until one day, because of that, until finally, and now."
  - before_after_belief: "Write the audience belief before and after in one sentence each."
  - pressure_point: "Ask what makes the message hard, not just what makes it nice."
  - voice_contrast: "Compare one right and one wrong reference by rhythm, density, attitude, and sincerity."
  - trope_kill_list: "List the obvious story shapes that would make the work generic."

facilitator_moves:
  - "Challenge pretty language that does not change the audience."
  - "Keep story anchored to truth, mechanics, or proof instead of generic inspiration."
  - "Separate story arc from final copy; do not write copy before the contract is clear."
  - "Preserve rejected arcs and voice anti-patterns for future agents."
  - "Route broader taste uncertainty to creative-session and option selection to concept-selection."

quality_bar:
  - "The artifact names audience shift, pressure, arc, voice, proof, rejected paths, and next workflow."
  - "The story has a reason to exist beyond sounding polished."
  - "A future agent can write, design, or specify from the compact story contract."

anti_patterns:
  - "Do not produce inspirational mush with no audience shift."
  - "Do not confuse lore volume with narrative usefulness."
  - "Do not erase rejected tropes after selection."
  - "Do not invent proof points; mark unknowns and route research."

paths:
  fast_path: "Write the audience shift, story spine, voice constraints, proof points, and reject list."
  deep_path: "Run creative-session, storytelling, concept-selection, and write-spec in sequence."

checkpoint_options:
  - creative-session
  - concept-selection
  - write-spec
  - domain-scan
  - council-decision

artifact_rules:
  Persist audience, before/after belief, pressure, arc beats, voice, proof points, rejected paths, assumptions, and next workflow.

domain_examples:
  - brand_story: "Explain why a product matters without defaulting to generic founder-story language."
  - campaign: "Shape tension, promise, proof, and payoff before copywriting."
  - game_narrative: "Bind premise, player role, world rules, and playable beats."
  - product_spec: "Turn narrative promise into constraints for PRD or UX."

headless:
  Draft a compact story contract from supplied material. Mark invented or weak proof as assumptions and route to research when needed.
