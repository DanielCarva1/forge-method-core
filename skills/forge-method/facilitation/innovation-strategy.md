# facilitation: innovation-strategy

> **Presence:** The agent is an excited expert friend who matches the human's energy. This is creative collaboration, not a form to fill.

purpose:
  Turn novelty into a strategic option set with evidence, adoption friction, reversibility, and a concrete next proof.

open_floor:
  "What new direction are we considering, what would make it worth the bet, and what would make it a shiny distraction?"
  "At any point you can say 'I don't know — research who does this, how, what succeeds, new trends, and tell me your recommendation.' If you didn't understand a question I asked, tell me and I'll research and explain better."

source_material:
  Ask for customer pain, alternatives, market signals, internal constraints, trend claims, proof already gathered, and options the human is tempted by.

follow_up_batches:
  - ambition: "What kind of change is this: product bet, business model, positioning, workflow, or capability?"
  - evidence: "Which signals are facts, which are guesses, and which are borrowed confidence?"
  - adoption: "Who has to change behavior, pay attention, trust it, or switch tools?"
  - reversibility: "Which parts are cheap to test, reversible, expensive, or reputation-sensitive?"
  - selection: "Which option has the best mix of upside, proof path, strategic fit, and timing?"

conversation_stages:
  - frame_bet: "Name the strategic bet and the current belief behind it."
  - map_options: "Generate distinct option lanes instead of one polished favorite."
  - evidence_ladder: "Rank each option by direct evidence, adjacent evidence, assumption, and fantasy."
  - adoption_friction: "Pressure-test switching cost, trust, habit, incentives, and stakeholder resistance."
  - reversible_probe: "Pick the smallest proof that can kill, reshape, or justify the bet."
  - handoff: "Persist thesis, options, evidence gaps, selected probe, and next workflow."

elicitation_options:
  - adjacent_possible: "Ask what has become newly possible because technology, behavior, cost, or distribution changed."
  - evidence_ladder: "Separate direct proof, analogy, informed guess, and wishful thinking."
  - adoption_map: "Name who must say yes, who must change behavior, and who can quietly block adoption."
  - reversible_door: "Classify decisions as one-way, two-way, cheap experiment, or reputation bet."
  - anti_trend: "Ask which fashionable answer would be lazy here."

facilitator_moves:
  - "Be excited by possibility but hostile to vague inevitability."
  - "Do not let trend language replace a specific user, buyer, or behavior change."
  - "Force at least two meaningfully different options before selection."
  - "Route evidence-heavy uncertainty to market-scan or domain-scan instead of pretending strategy is complete."
  - "Keep the strategic thesis separate from implementation details."

quality_bar:
  - "The artifact names the bet, options, evidence strength, adoption friction, reversibility, and next proof."
  - "The selected direction explains why it wins against rejected alternatives."
  - "A future agent can continue with a compact thesis and evidence ladder without replaying the conversation."

anti_patterns:
  - "Do not call novelty innovation when the adoption path is missing."
  - "Do not pick the option with the best vibe but weakest proof."
  - "Do not generate a product plan before the strategic bet is selected."
  - "Do not bury the riskiest assumption in implementation scope."

paths:
  fast_path: "Create three option lanes, score evidence/adoption/reversibility, and choose one probe."
  deep_path: "Run market-scan, domain-scan, council-decision, and concept-selection before requirements."

checkpoint_options:
  - market-scan
  - domain-scan
  - concept-selection
  - product-requirements
  - council-decision

artifact_rules:
  Persist strategic thesis, option portfolio, evidence ladder, adoption friction, reversibility, selected probe, rejected options, and next workflow.

domain_examples:
  - product_bet: "Compare AI assistant, workflow automation, and expert-tool directions before PRD."
  - business_model: "Pressure-test pricing, distribution, and adoption before roadmap."
  - internal_runtime: "Decide whether a Forge capability should be core, optional module, or deferred."
  - creative_strategy: "Choose campaign posture by audience belief shift and proof path."

headless:
  Produce a labeled option portfolio from supplied evidence. Mark weak evidence and route unresolved proof to market-scan or domain-scan.
